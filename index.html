About

Family Vibes

  • Dr.

    Mayrose Kavura Majinge

    A Social Entrepreneur and Psychosocial Therapist, is the Founder and Executive Director of Family Vibes Foundation. With extensive experience spanning 25 years, she is a Mental Wellness Consultant, Emotional Intelligence Coach, author, and TV host. Dr. Majinge holds a PhD in Human Development, and has led initiatives globally including the Universal Mission to Combat Hatred.

  • Family Vibes

    Foundation

    Family Vibes Foundation is an International registered non-governmental organization No. ooNGO/R/3625 based in Tanzania that deals with Mental health prevention and psychological, and Social Support services (Talk therapy), these services are provided through “Mahusiano” & Family Clinic serving individuals, groups, institutions, people in family relationships, and the community at large.

  • Mission

    To support connection, inner healing, and thriving among Tanzania community members impacted by sexual, relationship, and gender-based violence through confidential, trauma-informed consultation, counselling, and outreach.

  • Vission

    To promote mental Wellness and psychosocial support at all levels of society and provide professional services to everyone in need by building and promoting positive mental health throughout the community and providing available, community-based, professional services to people with negative mental health and psychosocial challenges in Tanzania.

UNIVERSAL MISSION TO COMBAT HATRED (UMCH)

UMCH is committed to uncovering the root causes of hatred and promoting positive social change. Our mission is to foster understanding, tolerance, and compassion across communities. By addressing the sources of hatred and encouraging dialogue among diverse groups, UMCH strives to create a world where differences are celebrated, and all individuals can thrive. We believe that unchecked hatred can hinder social and economic growth. By tackling this issue from a holistic perspective, UMCH aims to encourage positive emotional development and make these values a normal part of society. This approach not only combats hatred but also promotes overall well-being and development.

FUND RISING

Dinner

On July 3, 2024, at Mlimani City in Dar es Salaam, a significant fundraising dinner was held to support the Universal Mission to Combat Hatred, themed "Hatred as the Contagious Disease and Public Health Threat." This event emphasized the critical need to address hatred, a toxic and rapidly spreading emotion that poses a severe threat to social and individual well-being. Hatred, defined as an intense dislike for something or someone, can either positively drive actions against corruption and violence or negatively fuel racism, religious intolerance, and gender abuse. When mismanaged, hatred disrupts social interactions and personal growth, becoming a societal disease that requires urgent intervention and therapy. During the dinner, development stakeholders united to explore strategies for tackling this dangerous emotion, understanding its impact on social relationships and economic growth. The event highlighted the importance of fostering a society free from hatred, where individuals can live harmoniously and healthily. The evening aimed to raise awareness and funds to support initiatives combating hatred, promoting a message of tolerance, understanding, and empathy. The ultimate goal is to create a safer, more inclusive world where every individual can thrive free from the destructive influence of hatred.

ZUIA CHUKI

Kampeni and talent search

In Mbeya, our recent main event under the theme "Zuia Chuki campeni, Tumia 4R Okoa Maisha" resonated deeply as we united to combat hatred and promote harmony using the 4R principles—Reconciliation, Resilience, Reform, and Rebuild—inspired by Her Excellency President Samia. These principles serve as vital pillars for fostering multiparty democracy and societal cohesion across Tanzania. The "Zuia Chuki Campaign in Mbeya" stands as a testament to our collective commitment to national unity and social transformation. By embracing reconciliation, resilience, reform, and rebuilding, we strive to create a future where every individual is respected and empowered, ensuring a harmonious and prosperous society in Mbeya and throughout Tanzania. As part of this campaign, we also hosted the "Zuia Chuki Talent Search" event, a platform dedicated to discovering and celebrating local talent. Participants from diverse backgrounds showcased their skills and creativity, competing in various categories including music, dance, poetry, and more. This event aimed not only to unearth hidden gems but also to promote unity and harmony through artistic expression.

MENTAL HEALTH AWARENESS

JKT

Our recent event with Jeshi la Kujenga Taifa (JKT) was dedicated to enhancing mental health awareness and promoting strategies to prevent hatred. This initiative, known as "Afya ya Akili," aimed to equip JKT members with the necessary tools and knowledge to maintain mental well-being and foster a culture of harmony and respect. they also received training on identifying and mitigating factors that contribute to hatred and conflict within their ranks and communities. By empowering JKT members with these vital skills and insights, the event aimed to strengthen unity and enhance productivity within the organization. The event underscored our commitment to supporting the mental health and social cohesion of JKT members, ensuring they are well-prepared to contribute positively to their communities and the nation as a whole.

NATIONAL MENTAL WELLNESS SYMPOSIUM

Therapy

On October 9, 2023, in Dodoma, we hosted the "KONGAMANO TIBA LA KITAIFA AFYA YA AKILI" with the theme "Thibiti Magonjwa Yako kwa Afya ya Akili," focusing on the critical role of mental health in overall well-being. The event emphasized the mantra "Maisha bila msongo wa mawazo inawezekana" (Life without stress is achievable), promoting strategies to manage and prevent mental health disorders. Esteemed speakers and mental health experts convened to share insights on recognizing symptoms, seeking treatment, and promoting mental wellness. Interactive workshops and panel discussions explored holistic approaches to mental health care, encouraging participants to prioritize self-care and build resilience. The conference aimed to empower attendees with practical tools and resources to maintain optimal mental health and lead fulfilling lives. The "National Mental Health Therapy Conference" in Dodoma served as a pivotal platform for advancing awareness and advocacy, reaffirming our commitment to ensuring that mental health remains a priority in national health agendas. Through collective efforts, we strive to create a society where every individual can thrive free from the burden of mental health challenges

NATIONAL MENTAL WELLNESS DAY

2022

On October 9, 2022, in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, the "Mahadhimisho ya Wiki ya Afya ya Akili" was celebrated with the motto "Afya ya akili kipaumbele cha wote" (Mental health is a priority for everyone). This event was a key moment to raise awareness and highlight the importance of mental well-being in the community. The commitment to removing negative views about mental health and making support accessible for everyone was reinforced. By promoting the motto "Afya ya akili kipaumbele cha wote," the event aimed to create a culture where mental health is a top priority, ensuring that everyone feels supported and valued.

MEETING WITH HON. TULIA ACKSON

Advancing the Global Mission to Combat Hatred

On November 2, 2023, we had the privilege of meeting with Hon. Dr. Tulia Ackson to present and discuss our ambitious global initiative to combat hatred.
